Why youngest learners should return to school buildings first

By simulating King County coronavirus transmission patterns, researchers drew predictions about COVID-19 case numbers in schools under various reopening scenarios. The analysis suggests that when community transmission is under control, youngest learners should return to school buildings first, in part because some research hints that these children are less susceptible to infection than their older peers. Percentages represent the share of students, staff and teachers who, according to researchers' modeling, might step into school buildings with active COVID-19 infections from Sept. 1 through Dec. 1.

If this value is greater than 1, cases will increase exponentially. The number of cases per 100,000 residents represents different 14-day averages of new cases leading up to Sept. 1, 2020.
