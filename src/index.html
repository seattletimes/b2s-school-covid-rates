Back to school scenarios have trade offs

Simulating King County, researchers were able to draw predictions about how COVID-19 would affect schools in various scenarios. Based on community transmission levels, and with the assumption of decreasing rates, they found that not all back-to-school plans are created equal. Percentages represent the number of infectious people who would have stepped into school from Sept. 1 through Dec. 1. Those who who tested positive at home and kept from school are not included.
